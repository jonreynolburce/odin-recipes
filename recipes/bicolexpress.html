Spicy and Creamy Bicol Express

Bicol Express is a spicy, creamy, and flavorful Filipino dish made with pork cooked in coconut milk and lots of chili peppers. It’s inspired by the bold flavors of the Bicol region, known for its love of spicy food and coconut-based dishes. This recipe packs heat and richness in every bite.

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g pork belly, sliced into thin strips
  • 1 can coconut milk (400ml)
  • 1 can coconut cream (optional, for thicker sauce)
  • 3–5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, sliced
  • 5–10 green chilies (siling haba), sliced diagonally
  • 2–3 red chilies (siling labuyo), chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tbsp shrimp paste (bagoong alamang)
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt to taste

DIRECTIONS

  1. Heat oil in a pan and sauté garlic, onion, and ginger until aromatic.
  2. Add the pork and cook until slightly browned and fat is rendered.
  3. Stir in the shrimp paste and cook for 2–3 minutes.
  4. Pour in the coconut milk and bring to a simmer. Let it cook for 15–20 minutes until pork is tender.
  5. Add the green and red chilies. Simmer for another 5 minutes.
  6. Pour in the coconut cream (if using) and let it simmer until the sauce thickens.
  7. Season with salt to taste. Adjust spiciness depending on your preference.
  8. Serve hot with steamed rice (and maybe a glass of water).
