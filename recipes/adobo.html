Famous Chicken Adobo Recipe

Filipino Adobo is a classic Pinoy comfort food that everyone loves. It is usually made with chicken or pork, slowly cooked in a mix of soy sauce, vinegar. Every family has their own version of adobo, and that is what makes it special. Whether it is a bit sweeter, more garlicky, or even with a twist like adding coconut milk, adobo is always a crowd favorite and a true taste of home.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 kg chicken or pork (or a mix)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup of vinegar
  • crushed garlic
  • sliced onion
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar (Optional: for slight sweetness)
  • 1/2 of water (adjust if needed)
  • Cooking oil
  • Salt to taste

DIRECTIONS

  1. Marinate the meat in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, peppercorns, and bay leaves for at least 30 minutes (or overnight for more flavor).
  2. In a pan, heat a little oil and sauté garlic (and onion if using) until fragrant.
  3. Add the marinated meat (reserve the marinade) and brown the pieces on all sides.
  4. Pour in the marinade and add water. Bring to a boil.
  5. Lower the heat and simmer for 30–40 minutes (until meat is tender and sauce is reduced). Stir occasionally.
  6. Optional: Add sugar for a slight sweet balance. Adjust salt or vinegar depending on your taste.
  7. Let the sauce thicken to your preferred consistency.
  8. Serve hot with steamed rice.
